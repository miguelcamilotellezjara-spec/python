2. ABSTRACT FORMAL (Resumen del Paper_5.docx / Anisotropic Topology Framework - Trilingual Clean Version_6):
"Current paradigms in 3D generative modeling and digital simulation treat objects as isotropic, uniform geometric shells, failing to account for the internal structural physics of heterogeneous material removal (subtractive manufacturing) and anatomical tension (tensegrity). This paper introduces a unified computational framework that mathematicalizes classical Renaissance artistic principles to solve these bottlenecks. First, we propose a stress-aware Finite Element Method (FEM) that simulates anisotropic fracturing in heterogeneous solids, modeling tool-impact propagation through crystalline structures (e.g., stone carving). Second, we introduce an anatomically constrained neural network prior that embeds classical human figure-study parameters—specifically muscle volume conservation and skeletal weight distribution (contrapposto)—directly into the loss function of generative 3D diffusion models. Finally, we implement a formal verification layer using abstract interpretation to mathematically guarantee that generated structures conform to strict structural and aesthetic harmony constraints. By unifying material systems, computer vision, and formal methods, this framework provides a verifiable, physically grounded approach to next-generation generative design and robotic interaction."
